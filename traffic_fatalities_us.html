Introduction

Every year the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) releases a massive set of data collected for each recorded vehicle crash in the United States of America. NHTSA uses data from many sources, including the Fatality Analysis Reporting System (FARS) which began operation in 1975. Providing data about fatal crashes involving all types of vehicles, the FARS is used to identify highway safety problem areas, provide a basis for regulatory and consumer information initiatives, and form the basis for cost and benefit analyses of highway safety initiatives. The data is made available for public and academic researches with the goal of disclosing insights and connections inside the data. At the end it shall increase awareness and decrease fatalities.
This data visualization shall give some introduction to the traffic accident trends throughout year 2015.
